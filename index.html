Generations - Cards

First generation

(1996-1999)

The original Pokemon games are Japanese role-playing video games (RPGs) with an element of strategy, and were created by Satoshi Tajiri for the Game Boy. The Pokemon series began with the release of Poket Monsters Red and Green for the Game Boy in Japan. When these games proved popular, an enhanced Blue version was reprogrammed as Pokemon Red and Blue for international release. The original Green version was not released outside Japan

Thrid generation

(2002-2006)

Pokemon entered its third generation with the 2002 release of Pokemon Ruby and Saaphire for Game Boy Advance and continued with the Game Boy Advance remakes of Pokemon Red and Green, Pokemon FireRed and LeafGreen (Red and Green representing the original Japanese first generation games; territoreis outside Japan instead saw relese of Red and Blue). An enhance remake of Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire titled Pokemon Emerald followed after.

Second generation

(1999-2002)

The second generation of Pokemon video games gegan in 1999 with the Japanese releae of Pokemon Gold and Silver for the Game Boy Color, with Australia and North America getting the game in October 2000 and European release date of April 2001. Like the previous generation, as enhanced version, titled Pokemon Crystal, was later released.

Fourth generation

(2006-2010)

In 2006, Japan began the fourth generation of the franchise with the relese of Pokemon Deamond and Pearl for Nintendo DS. The games were released in North America on April 22, 2007 and in Australia on June 21, 2007. The game was reseased in the UK and Europe on July 27, 2007. Other main series gmes in the fourth generation include Pokemon Platinum, a diretor's cut version of Deamond and Pearl in the same vein as Pokemon Yellow, Crystal, and Emerald.It was released for the Nitendo DS in Japan on September 13, 2008, in North America on March 22, 2009, and in Australia and Europe on May 14, 2009 and May 22, 2009
